Target Audience Definition

"1M highly engaged users" is an outcome, not an audience. Define exactly WHO those people are.

0%

2.1 Buying Power Parameters

⚠️
High buying power ≠ "rich people." It means people with disposable income AND willingness to spend on digital entertainment and creator products.

Think about who can afford $5-25/mo subs, $50+ merch, and impulse donations. Students engage but rarely spend.

to

Average US gamer spends ~$76/mo. Twitch viewers average $15-30/mo on subs alone.

2.2 Demographics

to
to

English-speaking markets have highest ad CPMs. US/CA viewers worth 3-5x international for sponsors.

e.g., Los Angeles, Austin, London, Toronto, San Francisco — think where your niche audience clusters.

Tech and finance workers are highest-spending Twitch demographics by disposable income.

2.3 Psychographics

e.g., Razer, Nike, Red Bull, Logitech, Corsair, GFuel, Spotify, Apple, NZXT, HyperX

These define the "neighborhood" you're moving into — your audience already lives here.

2.4 Platform Behavior Profile

Average Twitch viewer watches ~95 min/day. Core fans do 2-4 hrs/day across platforms.

Most Twitch traffic peaks 6-11pm local time. Weekday mornings and late nights are underserved niches.

2.5 Content Vertical Selection

🌊
Blue Ocean Strategy: Instead of competing in a red ocean (saturated category), find the intersection of two verticals that nobody else occupies.

Red ocean example: "Fortnite gameplay." Blue ocean example: "Cooking while speedrunning retro games" or "Finance education through gaming analogies."
